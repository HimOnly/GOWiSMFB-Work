SEM Grant Empowerment

27, 000 benefit from empowerment programme.

  • September 5, 2018
  • By GOWiS Soluxions

No fewer than 27,000 Nigerians drawn from the 36 states of the federation have received grants from Vision Skills Development Centre, a non-governmental organisation (NGO).

The Chief Executive Officer of the NGO, Mr Israel Emeka Kama revealed this in Akure, the Ondo State capital during a meeting with beneficiaries of the NGO’s loans scheme.

Kama, who expressed displeasure on the economic situation of the country, said government alone cannot empower the citizens, hence the introduction of economic empowerment scheme by the organisation.

Although he praised Federal Government’s initiative towards empowering the less-privileged people through various schemes, especially the N-Power, he said the scheme could not take adequate care of all Nigerians. He, therefore, called on well-meaning Nigerians to collaborate with the government to fight poverty through economic empowerment for the less-privileged people.

Kama informed that the NGO was approved by the Social Exchange Market (SEM), adding that it is also being funded by the agency, with the aim of making all Nigerians economically self-reliant.

He said the NGO would source for grant for the beneficiaries of the scheme through the SEM and also monitor the progress of the businesses carried out with the grant to ensure that the purpose for which it was given was justified.

He also hinted that the NGO has gathered entrepreneurs across the country to train beneficiaries of the grant in various entrepreneurial skills, just as he informed that various programmes aimed at developing small and medium-scale businesses will be encouraged by the NGO.

“We are determined to make people grow economically; we are determined to make positive impacts on the lives of the people through our intervention programmes. To this end, we are ready to fund businesses ranging from N250, 000 to N10 million through grants,” he stated.

Kama assured that all individuals who applied for the NGO’s grant will be duly considered, even as he informed that the disbursement of the money will hold before this year ends.

He, however, dismissed insinuations that the scheme was a scam, saying that the Economic and Financial Crimes Commission (EFCC) is fully aware of the activities of the NGO.
